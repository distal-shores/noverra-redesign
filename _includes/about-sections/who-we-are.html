The Noverra Group of Companies was founded and is led by three equal Owners, Omar Ladak, Steve Lux and Suzanne Boyd.

Omar Ladak, Owner

Omar is responsible for ensuring strategic alignment between Noverra’s three Owners and our Operating Company Executives. In addition, he works with Steve on the evaluation of new acquisitions to fuel the grown of our group; and with Suzanne on the creation of Leadership Development programing to proactively support the growth of our people. Further he provides mentorship and leadership development support for our most senior Operating Company Executives.

Omar was formerly a Senior Partner and Member of the Board of Directors at RPO Management Consultants, a boutique strategy consultancy with a national Canadian practice which was merged with the Secor Group and ultimately acquired by KPMG Canada.

Omar has been a principal, business owner, board director or advisory board member of companies in the Business Services, Financial Services, Technology, Real-Estate and Construction Services industries. His consulting advisory experience spanned the Telecommunications, Transportation, Financial Services, Healthcare, Technology, Manufacturing, Distribution, Alternative Energy, and Government sectors.

In addition to his business and entrepreneurial pursuits, Omar is passionate about the community and has been actively involved through board and advisory roles in various not-for-profit organizations. Omar has served as a member of the Board, Advisory Board or Campaign Cabinet for the United Way of the Lower Mainland; the Quest Food Exchange; The Centre for Healthcare Management at the Sauder School of Business; and the BC Construction Safety Alliance.

Omar is an active member of the Young Presidents Organization (YPO) and currently serves on the Executive Board for the YPO Chapter in British Columbia.

Omar graduated with an undergraduate degree in Engineering from the University of British Columbia (UBC), as well as a Masters’ degree in Business Administration from the Sauder Business School at UBC. In addition, he has completed the President’s Program at Harvard Business School as well as executive education programs in corporate finance, corporate growth, leading business schools including, INSEAD; and; the Kellogg School of Business at Northwestern University.

Steve Lux, Owner

Steve Lux is responsible for the Financial Strategy and Management of the Group as well as Performance Reporting for each of our Operating Companies. In addition, Steve leads the evaluation process for potential new acquisitions, the primary driver of our growth strategy.

Prior to co-founding Noverra, Steve’s experience includes senior advisory roles at RPO Management Consultants and PricewaterhouseCoopers. Steve has almost 20 years of financial and strategic advisory experience having advised clients across Canada, the United States, and internationally on operational, business and financial strategy. His work prior to co-founding Noverra included CFO level roles for early stage and mid-sized companies as well as advisory work to senior executives in multi-billion dollar organizations. His experience spans a broad base of industries including: healthcare, insurance, financial services, government, technology, investment management, and construction services; and includes a broad set of operational, financial and strategic responsibilities.

Steve currently serves as the Vice Chair of the Board for the Independent Contractors and Business Association of BC. In addition, he serves as the Chair of the Audit Committee and a Member of the Board for Benefits Services Ltd., a privately held benefits services company providing health benefits management services for small and medium sized construction focused companies in British Columbia.

Steve is a Certified Professional Accountant (CPA) who holds a n MBA from the Rotman School of Management at the University of Toronto, where he was awarded the Roger N. Wolff Prize and the Andrew Alexander Kinghorn Award as Rotman’s top student in both years of the MBA program. In addition, Steve holds a Bachelor of Commerce degree from the University of Alberta.

Suzanne Boyd, Owner

Suzanne is responsible for the development and delivery of Leadership Programs across the Noverra Group of Companies. In addition, Suzanne provides expert level support for Human Resources opportunities and challenges within our Operating Companies across: recruitment, work force planning, organizational design, and performance management.

Prior to co-founding Noverra, Suzanne was the Principal of EdgeStone HR Consulting, a leading Vancouver based HR advisory practice. Prior to her consulting career, Suzanne was a strategic project leader for Vancity, a Vancouver based retail and commercial banking company; as well as an Executive Search Professional at Ray & Berndtson (now Boyden Global Executive Serach), a leading retained executive search firm.

Suzanne’s experience has spanned leadership development and corporate change programs across a broad set of industries including: primary resources, manufacturing, construction, bio-technology, transportation, utilities, retail banking, insurance, government, and the not-for-profit sector. Her expertise focuses on: leadership and management development as well as organizational change management to enable growth and operational effectiveness improvement. In addition she has strong experience in human resources program development and implementation for recruitment, performance management, workforce planning, and core HR practices.

In addition to her business pursuits, Suzanne is actively engaged in the community as a member of the Board of Governors of Shawinigan Lake School, her alma mater, where she has served as a member of the Governance Committee and the Leadership Succession Committee, as well as Chair of the Human Resources Committee.

Suzanne is a Certified Human Resources Professional (CHRP) and graduate of the Executive Masters of Business Administration program at Simon Fraser University (SFU). In addition, she earned her undergraduate degree in Sociology and Women’s Studies from the University of British Columbia.
